# Human-Factors, Labeling, Training, and Competency Execution

## Use specification and participant control

Approve the user groups, responsibilities, training/experience, physical/cognitive characteristics,
environment, workflow, frequency, critical tasks, reasonably foreseeable misuse, abnormal use and
failure recovery for the frozen intended use. Recruitment must represent actual pathologists,
laboratory personnel and administrators across intended site types; developers and expert reviewers
cannot substitute for representative users.

Participant identity is retained in the controlled study system. Repository records use coded IDs
only. Record inclusion/exclusion, role/experience, training received, conflicts, environment and
deviations. Do not coach beyond the approved IFU/training during summative validation.

## Formative execution

Observe real or realistic end-to-end work: case/input selection, suitability and identity checks,
source/overlay comparison, dense/artifact/edge review, interpretation of outputs, acceptance/
rejection, report/provenance, complaints, authentication/readiness/audit/model failure, downtime and
recovery. Include unsupported stains/specimens/scanners/resolution, misleading but plausible output,
interruption, time pressure and wrong-case scenarios.

For every error, close call, confusion, assistance, delay or workaround, record task, observation,
root cause, potential harm, affected use-related risk, design/label/training correction, verification
and residual risk. Conduct iterative cycles until critical hazards are controlled by design and
workflow where practicable. Formative data are not counted as summative successes.

## Summative execution

Freeze product, release/model, environment, IFU and training before the study. The approved protocol
predefines sample rationale and strata, scenarios, randomization, task success, assistance,
comprehension, use-error and close-call definitions, criticality, stopping rules, missing/deviation
handling and analysis. Independent facilitators capture raw observations and exact participant
actions without reinterpretation during collection.

Every critical task must be attempted by every applicable participant. Any critical use error or
close call receives root-cause and clinical-risk assessment. Release requires zero unexplained or
unmitigated critical use error; a product, IFU or training change triggers impact assessment and
repeat of affected validation. Report all participants, exclusions, failures, assistance and
deviations.

## Labeling and training release

Regulatory, Clinical Safety, Usability and Quality reconcile the final IFU, UI, reports, warnings,
supported-input table, contraindications, output interpretation, performance, residual risks,
downtime, complaint reporting and cybersecurity instructions against authorization and evidence.
Screenshots and identifiers must match the production release. Translations receive qualified
review and comprehension validation where required.

Training includes common and dangerous failure examples, not only successful cases. Competence
requires observed critical-task performance and an approved knowledge threshold. Record each
authorization in [USER_COMPETENCY_REGISTER.csv](USER_COMPETENCY_REGISTER.csv). Failed users retrain
and reassess; access remains disabled until competent. Reassessment occurs after material change,
prolonged inactivity, incident, repeated rejection/error, audit finding or expiry.

## Exit evidence

Exit requires approved use specification and task analysis, closed formative findings, frozen and
passing summative report, evidence-consistent authorized labeling, validated training effectiveness,
competent-user records and site integration. An attendance sheet alone does not establish competence.
