# SOP: Suppliers, Internal Audit and Management Review

Supplier owners classify vendors by effect on safety, performance, compliance, privacy, security
and availability. Before use, Quality approves requirements, competence/certification evidence,
security/privacy terms, change and incident notification, audit rights, records, continuity and exit
plan. Critical suppliers include cloud/identity/logging, container and package sources, scanners,
annotation/clinical sites, laboratories, contract developers, penetration testers and eQMS. Ongoing
monitoring uses defects, uptime, vulnerabilities, audit findings and change performance. Material
failure triggers containment, re-evaluation and CAPA.

Quality maintains a risk-based audit program covering all QMS processes over the approved cycle.
Auditors are competent and do not audit their own work. Plans define scope, criteria, samples and
prior findings. Reports distinguish conformities, observations and graded nonconformities, identify
objective evidence and owners/dates, and verify corrective-action effectiveness. Audit reports and
supplier-audit records are maintained inspection-ready.

Executive management reviews the QMS at planned intervals and after material safety events. Inputs
include prior actions, regulatory/standards changes, audits, complaints/vigilance, CAPA,
postmarket/model drift, cybersecurity/privacy, supplier performance, training, process/product
metrics, resource/competence and improvement opportunities. Outputs assign decisions, resources,
quality-objective changes, product/process actions and owners/dates. Quality tracks closure.

Required records: supplier classification/evaluation/agreement/monitoring, approved supplier list,
audit program/plans/reports/closures, management-review agenda/input pack/minutes/action log.
