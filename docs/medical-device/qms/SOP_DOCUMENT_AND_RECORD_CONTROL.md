# SOP: Document, Record and Training Control

1. The author requests a document ID from Quality and states purpose, scope, owner, reviewers,
   affected products and required training. Quality checks the approved template and classification.
2. Reviewers verify technical, clinical, regulatory, privacy and security content as applicable.
   The approver is independent of authorship where required. Approval records bind content hash,
   revision and effective date.
3. Quality publishes the effective copy, withdraws superseded points of use, preserves prior
   revisions, and updates the master index. Unapproved drafts carry an unmistakable watermark/status.
4. Changes use a controlled request with reason, affected records/products/sites, impact assessment,
   review, approval, effective date and retraining. Emergency changes are time-limited and receive
   retrospective review within the approved period.
5. Records are completed contemporaneously, legibly and attributable. Blank fields are marked not
   applicable with rationale. Corrections never obscure originals. Attachments receive identifiers
   and hashes. Quality protects records against alteration, loss and unauthorized disclosure.
6. The record schedule defines minimum retention from product life/last placement, plus longer
   jurisdictional, contractual, clinical and privacy requirements. Legal hold suspends deletion.
7. Role owners map required procedures to personnel. Training includes revision, method, due date,
   assessment and trainer approval. Work is not authorized until competence is demonstrated.
8. Quality reviews overdue training, access, backup/restore evidence and document-index accuracy
   monthly; deviations enter nonconformance/CAPA.

Required records: document/change request, review comments, signed approval, distribution and
withdrawal log, training/competence record, retention schedule, access review and restore test.
