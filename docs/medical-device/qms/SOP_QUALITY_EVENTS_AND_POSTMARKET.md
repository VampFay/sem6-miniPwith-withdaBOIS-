# SOP: Nonconformance, CAPA, Complaints, Vigilance and Field Action

All staff and suppliers immediately record suspected product, process, privacy, cybersecurity or
quality failures. Intake assigns a unique ID, source, affected versions/sites, event time, discovery
time, patient involvement, description, evidence preservation and reporter contact. Clinical safety
performs immediate harm/continued-use triage; Security and Privacy join relevant events. Containment
may disable analysis, quarantine artifacts, revoke access, advise downtime or suspend distribution.

Quality determines whether the event is a nonconformance, complaint, adverse event, security/privacy
incident or combination. Regulatory documents jurisdiction-specific reportability and deadline from
the first awareness date; uncertainty is escalated, not used to delay assessment. Complaints receive
investigation unless a documented, approved rationale shows one is unnecessary. Returned artifacts
and logs are handled under consent, privacy and chain-of-custody rules.

CAPA is opened for systemic, recurring, severe, reportable or trend-significant issues. The owner
defines the problem and scope, performs evidence-based root-cause analysis, evaluates similar
products/processes, proposes corrections and preventive actions, assesses new risks, validates
changes, sets effectiveness criteria and due dates, and obtains independent approval. Effectiveness
is checked after enough exposure; failure reopens/escalates CAPA.

The legal manufacturer controls advisory notices, field safety corrective action, removal/recall,
customer/regulator communication, returned-product reconciliation and closure. Only authorized
spokespersons communicate externally. Quality trends events at least monthly during launch and at
the approved interval thereafter, feeding risk management and management review.

Required records: intake/complaint, triage and reportability decision, investigation, containment,
CAPA, effectiveness check, regulator reports/correspondence, customer communication, field-action
reconciliation and closure approval.
