# Regulatory Classification and Submission Execution

## Market-by-market control

Open one row in [MARKET_AUTHORIZATION_REGISTER.csv](MARKET_AUTHORIZATION_REGISTER.csv) for each
jurisdiction. Authorization, classification, registration, labeling and postmarket duties are not
transferred from another jurisdiction. The legal manufacturer and qualified local regulatory
professional approve every conclusion against the frozen intended purpose; repository authors do
not infer classification from implementation alone.

## Decision package

For the initial market, compile:

- legal manufacturer, establishments, local representative/importer/economic operators and QMS
  scope;
- exact intended purpose, indications, specimen/patient population, users, environment, inputs,
  outputs, exclusions, contraindications, clinical-decision significance and automation level;
- product/device/IVD/accessory status, classification rule/product code and complete rationale;
- predicate/equivalence or novelty analysis, submission/conformity pathway, authority/notified-body
  and ethics interactions, meeting questions, written feedback and binding conditions;
- applicable laws, guidance, consensus/harmonized standards and edition-specific deviations;
- claim-to-evidence matrix covering analytical/clinical performance or evaluation, benefit-risk,
  software, model/data, cybersecurity, privacy, usability, labeling and postmarket evidence;
- investigational/test authorization and clinical-study requirements before any patient-use study;
- registration, establishment, UDI, language, local labeling, distribution and import requirements;
  and
- change-notification rules for code, model/data, threshold, UI, intended use, infrastructure,
  supplier, cybersecurity correction and postmarket field action.

## Submission assembly and review

Create a controlled submission index mapping every authority requirement to a current approved
artifact, owner, reviewer, version, evidence hash and location. Regulatory performs completeness and
consistency review across intended use, claims, clinical protocols/results, risk controls, software
requirements, cybersecurity, IFU, training and postmarket plans. Contradictory versions, unsupported
claims, missing raw-data access, undisclosed anomalies or unresolved critical/high risks stop
submission.

Authority/notified-body questions enter a controlled deficiency record with awareness/response due
dates, root issue, affected documents/claims/risks, cross-functional owner, approved response and
commitment tracking. A response never changes the device, evidence interpretation or labeling
outside design/change control.

## Authorization and launch conditions

Record the exact authorized intended purpose, model/release scope, conditions, certificate/order/
license identifier, effective/expiry dates, establishment and UDI status, approved labeling,
distribution limitations, required studies/reports and change obligations. Quality independently
verifies that the production release and every site match authorization. Marketing, sales, support
and training materials receive regulatory review before distribution.

## Exit evidence

Exit requires a qualified written pathway decision, completed authority/notified-body interaction,
accepted submission, effective authorization/registration, approved labeling and UDI/establishment
records as applicable, fulfilled prelaunch conditions, and operational reporting/change-control
obligations. A submission receipt or regulator meeting is not market authorization.
