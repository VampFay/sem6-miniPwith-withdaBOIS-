# Postmarket Surveillance and Response Execution

## Prelaunch activation

Quality activates a single controlled intake across support, sales, clinical, security and site
channels. All staff are trained to recognize a complaint, incident, near miss, use error, privacy or
cybersecurity event even when the reporter uses different language. Publish staffed contacts and
deputies, including a 24/7 route for potential serious harm, active exploitation, integrity loss or
unavailable safe workflow.

Regulatory maintains the current market-specific reportability matrix: awareness definition,
authority/contact, serious/public-health criteria, deadline, form, follow-up, trend/periodic reporting
and field-action obligations. Timers begin at awareness and are not delayed for complete information
or root cause.

## Baseline, inventory, and monitoring

Before first distribution, freeze release/model/configuration/labeling and validation/PQ baselines,
installed site/version inventory, exposure denominators, expected input/failure distributions,
clinically justified performance/drift measures, alert/action thresholds and review cadence.
Telemetry is privacy approved, pseudonymous, version/site attributable, and excludes pixels/direct
identifiers. Unsupervised drift is a triage signal, not evidence of clinical performance change.

## Event execution

Open each complaint, incident, signal, vulnerability, privacy event, use error, field action or CAPA
in [POSTMARKET_EVENT_REGISTER.csv](POSTMARKET_EVENT_REGISTER.csv) and the validated eQMS. Preserve
awareness time, affected release/site/scope, exposure denominator, patient impact, evidence and chain
of custody. Immediately assess containment/stop-use, clinical harm, privacy/security, reportability,
related events and affected sites/versions.

Investigation uses locked definitions and independent review; it does not tune the production model
or silently discard inconvenient cases. Root cause and systemic extent update risk, clinical,
usability, cybersecurity/privacy and supplier files. Actions may include monitoring, correction,
CAPA, training/labeling, site requalification, controlled new release/model, customer advisory,
field correction/recall or suspension. Verify effectiveness and recurrence before closure.

## Exercises and governance

Before launch, execute complaint-to-vigilance, serious near-miss, active vulnerability, privacy
disclosure, wrong-model/site-integrity and recall simulations. Demonstrate intake recognition,
clock start, cross-functional triage, site inventory/reconciliation, regulator/customer messages,
containment, recovery, evidence preservation, CAPA and management escalation within approved targets.

Quality and Clinical Safety review operational/security signals at least weekly during initial
launch and conduct the approved periodic aggregate review. Formal reports reconcile exposure,
complaints/incidents, trends, drift/reference reviews, CAPA/field actions, cybersecurity, literature,
regulatory changes and benefit-risk. Stop-use and restart decisions require documented authority.

## Exit evidence

Exit requires staffed intake/on-call, reporting matrix, validated eQMS workflows, privacy-approved
telemetry, approved baseline/thresholds, current installed-base inventory, supplier/security feeds,
periodic-report schedule, working patch/field-action capability, passed incident and recall exercises,
and management approval. A blank register or written plan is not postmarket readiness.
