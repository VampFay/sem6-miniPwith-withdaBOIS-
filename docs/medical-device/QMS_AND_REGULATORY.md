# Quality System and Regulatory Work Plan

The legal manufacturer and target markets are not yet defined. Before design freeze, obtain
qualified regulatory counsel and determine device/software classification, submission route,
clinical evidence expectations, cybersecurity obligations, privacy regime, laboratory integration
requirements, and whether use constitutes laboratory-developed testing in each jurisdiction.

Establish a functioning quality management system before regulated development evidence is relied
upon. At minimum control: quality manual and responsibilities; training/competence; document and
record control; design/development planning and reviews; user needs and design inputs/outputs;
risk management; software lifecycle/configuration/problem resolution; supplier qualification;
verification/validation and clinical evaluation; usability engineering; cybersecurity; data and AI
model lifecycle; change control; release and distribution; complaints/vigilance; CAPA; audits;
management review; and postmarket surveillance.

Create a design history/technical file with approved intended use, regulatory strategy, software
safety classification, architecture/interfaces, SOUP inventory, requirements/traceability, risk
file, usability file, cybersecurity file/SBOM, dataset/model provenance, verification records,
clinical evidence, labeling, installation/service procedures, release records, and unresolved
anomaly assessment. Repository templates are working documents only; the eQMS remains the system
of record with signatures, versions, retention, and access controls.

No code completion can substitute for ethics approvals, representative specimens, qualified
clinical reviewers, independent verification, regulator interaction, site validation, legal
license clearance, or signed residual-risk acceptance.

## Current jurisdiction anchors

These are planning anchors, not a classification decision or legal opinion. The approved regulatory
strategy must identify the exact versions applicable to the frozen intended use and each launch
market:

- United States: FDA's [Quality Management System Regulation](https://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/postmarket-requirements-devices/quality-management-system-regulation-qmsr)
  became effective on 2026-02-02 and incorporates ISO 13485:2016 by reference. Premarket route,
  device classification, clinical evidence, cybersecurity, and change-control expectations still
  require a product-specific assessment.
- European Union: use the European Commission's current [MDCG endorsed guidance](https://health.ec.europa.eu/medical-devices-sector/new-regulations/guidance-mdcg-endorsed-documents-and-other-guidance_en),
  including MDCG 2019-11 rev.1 (June 2025), when assessing software qualification and classification
  under the MDR/IVDR; obtain a conformity-assessment strategy from the responsible regulatory lead.
- India: CDSCO states that software can fall within the medical-device definition and publishes a
  software classification notice and the [Medical Devices Rules, 2017](https://cdsco.gov.in/opencms/opencms/en/Acts-and-rules/Medical-Devices-Rules/).
  The legal manufacturer must obtain a written CDSCO classification/licensing strategy for the
  intended oncology-laboratory claims before clinical deployment.
